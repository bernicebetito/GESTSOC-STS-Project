Need for More Food

The ever growing population of humans has led into the demand for more food.

There are still people who are considered undernourished by the United Nations and it is estimated that around 690 million people or 8.9% of the world population experience hunger.

77% of agricultural land is dedicated to livestock while only 23% is for actual crops even though the overall daily protein and calorie provided by meat is much lower than that of what plants provide Our World in Data (2019).

Offshore Aquaculture

“Offshore mariculture, which does not depend on wild fish harvest, has the potential to become a primary food source to meet this demand. Despite an overall interest and justification in investments in offshore aquaculture, there are very few offshore cage designs suitable for use today." (Shainee et al., 2012)

Better Aquaculture System

Government Projects

Bureau of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources (BFAR)

BFAR maritime floating fish cages installed off-shore from Poblacion, Bohol, have yielded 936 kilograms of Bangus, with records indicating a profit of more than P200,000 from the harvests in 2021 compared to 2019 (Newman, 2021).

BFAR plans to establish 300 fish cages in every region of the country in order to enhance food production and safeguard food sustainability for the people. It aims to place cages in the ocean, or in tanks, ponds or raceways that have seawater contained in them. (Ison, 2019).

The BFAR has a target of increasing fish production in the next 5 years and promises to provide credit, technical assistance and support to fishermen interested in the venture (Ison, 2019).
